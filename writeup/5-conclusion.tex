\section{Conclusion}
We report on a mechanization of SAX and a mechanization of the proof of its translation to the sequent calculus. We also mechanize the proofs of cut-elimination, cut-admissibility, and the subformula property for Soprillo, a fragment of SAX containing only snips, implication, and the identity rule.
\subsection{Future Work}
The most obvious avenue for future work is to extend our mechanizations to fragments larger than Soprillo. While extending these proofs to the complete SAX system may be infeasible, mechanizations that include $\otimes$ would be of great use in showing the adaptability of our representations and proof techniques used. In a similar vein, a mechanization of SAX within adjoint logic would allow for similar meta-theoretic guarantees regarding the message-passing interpretation of SAX. Further, a mechanization of SNAX should follow relatively easily from our mechanization.

Another natural step is a mechanization of the shared memory interpretation of SAX, along with its properties including progress and termination.~\cite{DeYoung2020} presents a logical relations argument for the latter, which are particularly amenable to mechanization. We suspect that the dynamic semantics of the shared memory interpretation, which takes the form of multiset rewriting rules on configurations (allocated memory cells) can be represented as ordered lists in Beluga. While we intended to explore this path further, our initial challenges at properly representing eligibility in Beluga stalled the project significantly.
